## 3. Project 1: **ABC Group Global Forensic Investigation**

**Objective:**  
Investigate **whistleblower allegations** of **procurement fraud**, **vendor favoritism**, and **illicit enrichment** within the subsidiary operations of **ABC Group**.  

### 3.1 Whistleblower Deconstruction

- Systematically **mapped allegations** to specific **entities, transactions, and individuals**, identifying **suspicious relationships** between procurement managers and select vendors.  
- Used this mapping to prioritize **high-risk vendors**, unusual pricing patterns, and **repeat relationships** over time.

### 3.2 Wealth & Lifestyle Analysis

- Performed a **“Living Beyond Means”** audit on key individuals.  
- Identified a **$24.0 million personal asset portfolio** reconciled against a **$100,000 monthly salary**, highlighting potential **undisclosed income streams** and red flags typical of lifestyle audits in fraud cases.

### 3.3 Forensic Document Audit

- Conducted a detailed review of **Purchase Orders (POs)**, **invoices**, and related documents.  
- Uncovered an **impossible audit trail** where **invoices were backdated 4 days before the Purchase Order** was issued, combined with **currency mismatches** that suggested possible manipulation of pricing and settlement terms—hallmarks of sophisticated procurement fraud schemes.

### 3.4 Global Coordination

- Coordinated investigative workflows between teams in **India** and **China** to validate:  
  - The **legitimacy of overseas suppliers**,  
  - **Shipping and logistics logs**,  
  - Supporting documentation from multiple jurisdictions.
- This cross-border alignment strengthened the **evidentiary chain** and ensured **local regulatory nuances** were considered.

## 4. Project 2: **Technology Risk & IT Audit (PayNow Application)**

**Objective:**  
Evaluate the effectiveness of **IT General Controls (ITGCs)** across the **“PayNow”** application’s **change management lifecycle** to protect the **reliability of financial reporting**.  

### 4.1 Workflow Risk Analysis

Assessed the four stages of the **SDLC pipeline** to prevent **unauthorized code changes** in production:

1. **Initiation**  
   - Reviewed how **business units request changes**, focusing on documentation, approvals, and risk classification.  

2. **Development**  
   - Tested **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** to ensure developers **cannot directly modify production code** or bypass standard deployment pipelines.

3. **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)**  
   - Evaluated the **independence and competence** of testers and whether the **test environment** faithfully mirrors production, a key ITGC consideration.

4. **CCB Approval (Change Control Board)**  
   - Confirmed that the **CCB** only approves changes with **robust evidence** of successful testing and impact analysis, and that **no unapproved changes** are introduced after UAT. 

### 4.2 ITGC Framework Application

- Focused on **Manage Access** and **Manage Changes** controls to:  
  - Prevent developers from **bypassing protocols**.  
  - Ensure all changes are **authorized, tested, and traceable** from request to deployment.

### 4.3 Advanced Idea Mechanics (AIM)

- Designed **high-impact probing questions** (AIM) to validate:  
  - The **independence of software testers**,  
  - The **quality and sufficiency of evidence** presented for final migration approval,  
  - The robustness of logs and deployment records used to confirm **only approved code** reached production.

## 5. Methodological Philosophy: **Professional Skepticism**

Across both **forensic investigations** and **IT audit**, I apply a **critical thinking framework** to mitigate cognitive biases that can compromise judgment:

- **Confirmation Bias**  
  - Deliberately seek information that might **contradict existing beliefs or management narratives**, rather than only confirming them. 

- **Groupthink**  
  - Encourage **healthy conflict and challenge** within teams to ensure **individual responsibility** and prevent over-reliance on consensus.  

- **Availability Bias**  
  - Break down **complex problems into simpler components** so that data is **weighted on relevance** rather than **ease of access**, in line with System 2 thinking principles.

This philosophy underpins a **Professional Skepticism** mindset—maintaining a **questioning mind** and a **fact-based assessment** of evidence.
